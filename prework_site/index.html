Passion for Outdoor Recreation

Outdoor recreation has always been a passion of mine. As a young child my parents would take my brothers and I camping nearly every month and teach us to appreciate outdoor activities such as hiking and rafting. Prior to attending Turing, and my role as an operations manager, I was able to spend 3 years working in Yosemite National Park. During this time I worked for the the concessionaire of the park helping to facilitate tours and activities as well as promote the preservation of the park to daily visitors. When I was not working I spent time hiking, camping, climbing, and enjoying all the park had to offer.

Over the course of this summer after being accepted to Turing and becoming part of the 1808 cohort I was able to take some time to conintue pursue my passion of outdoor recreation. From June to August of this year, my girlfriend and I were able to visit several National Parks. Each park provided a unique experience to get outside. From the high temperatues and sand dunes of Death Valley to the high desert-red cliff environment of Zion National park, all the parks were places that visitors from all over the world visit to spend time admiring.

  • Yosemite
  • Death Valley
  • Zion
  • Bryce Canyon
  • Capitol Reef
  • Arches
  • Rocky Mountain
